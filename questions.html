SC Topography

South Carolina's topography is diverse, encompassing coastal plains, rolling hills, and the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains. The state features a predominantly low-lying coastal plain along the Atlantic Ocean, characterized by marshes, estuaries, and sandy beaches. Moving inland, the terrain transitions into gently rolling hills, particularly in the Piedmont region, before ascending to the more rugged and elevated areas of the Blue Ridge Mountains in the northwest. Rivers, including the Savannah, Edisto, and Pee Dee, traverse the state, contributing to its varied landscape and providing essential water resources for the region.
